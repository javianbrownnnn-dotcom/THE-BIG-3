# Christianity & Religious-History Documentary Research — Executive Summary

**Date:** July 11, 2026 · **Phase:** 1–5 complete · **Dataset:** 40 channels identified, 25 deep-dived · **Confidence:** High

## OVERVIEW
Second CI cycle, targeting the niche of our existing channel **Ancient Religions & Storytelling (Myth & Meaning)**. Full deliverables mirror the business cycle: working list, deep dives, pattern analysis, opportunities/threats/ideas, and a consolidated report.

## KEY FINDINGS
1. **Rigor and narrative are substitutes in this niche** — no channel combines scholarly sourcing with cinematic storytelling; the quadrant is empty across all 40 mapped channels.
2. **Patronage-first economics:** only 3/25 deep-dived channels are AdSense-led. Patreon/memberships dominate (0.13–0.35% of subs convert), courses are the proven premium tier (Ehrman: $50–300 courses off 220K subs), giants are donation-funded nonprofits.
3. **Artifact-led thumbnails beat faces** for documentary formats — the inverse of the business niche; non-clickbait packaging is a trust signal.
4. **Low cadence is viable:** six ~1.1M-sub channels ship 0.1–0.5 videos/week; watch time + patronage decouple income from frequency.
5. **Credentialed human narration is the moat:** 24/25 channels use zero AI; this audience checks citations and punishes errors permanently.

## TOP OPPORTUNITY
The **serialized early-Christianity narrative arc** (flagship) plus the **"Rise and Fall of a God" franchise** (quarterly tent-poles) — supported by the long-form graduation path: BibleProject, Dan McClellan, and OSP audiences (500K–1.5M reachable) have no long-form destination.

## TIME-SENSITIVE
**Mike Winger's June–August 2026 hiatus** pauses the niche's biggest long-form Bible catalog — Q3 window for our early-Christianity and manuscript episodes.

## THREATS TO MONITOR
Sensationalism trust-burn (the Gnostic Informant lesson) · scholarly-error risk · camp capture (stay neutral) · reaction-content trap · key-person burnout (19/25 channels are 1–2 people).

93K subs · 600K views/mo · $7,600/mo (AdSense $3.0K + Patreon $2.1K + courses $2.5K) — patronage + courses = 60% of revenue. Sponsors excluded as upside.
